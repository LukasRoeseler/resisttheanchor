THE ANCHORING EFFECT

Yes
Somewhat
No
Heads up: When people make numerical estimates, their guesses get pulled toward whatever number they just saw — even when they know the number is irrelevant, even when they try to ignore it. This is the anchoring effect, and it's surprisingly hard to beat.

In this game we're going to test whether you can resist it.

HOW TO PLAY

Ten rounds. Three lives. Try not to be fooled.

1. A random number between 0 and 2000 appears. It has nothing to do with anything — just look at it and press SPACE.

2. A cloud of dots flashes for 1 second. Look carefully.

3. Were there more or less dots than that random number? Press A for MORE, L for LESS.

4. Type your best estimate. Try not to let the random number influence you.

Estimate coins: within 40% → 1 coin · within 20% → 2 coins · within 10% → 3 coins.
Bonus: +1 coin if your more/less call was correct.
Lives: miss by more than 60% → lose a heart.
